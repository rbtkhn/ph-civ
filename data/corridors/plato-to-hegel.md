# Plato to Hegel

Plato to Hegel is the secondary Volume I theological-philosophical spine.

If [Homer to Tolstoy](homer-to-tolstoy.md) shows how civilization remembers itself through literary memory-forms, this route shows how civilization thinks about reality, sacred order, imagination, knowledge, and history.

Its thinker sequence is:

```text
Plato -> Augustine -> Dante -> Kant -> Hegel
```

This is the cleaner parallel spine for readers who enter Civilization through metaphysics, theology, and philosophy rather than through epic, theater, and the novel.

## Structural Invariant

Treat this route as a secondary Volume I spine, not as a replacement for Homer-to-Tolstoy. The literary route remains the primary public spine. This route gives the repo a second stable entrance into the same law-discovery semester.

## Why This Route Exists

The literary spine teaches how civilizations encode themselves in epic, drama, and the novel.

This theological-philosophical spine teaches how civilizations:

- distinguish reality from appearance
- justify spiritual authority or freedom
- imagine the relation between God and the human
- turn imagination into epistemology
- transform theology into philosophy of history

## Dante As Shared Hinge

Dante is not a duplication problem between the two Volume I spines. Dante is the major shared hinge.

In the literary spine, Dante is the transformation of epic inheritance.

In this spine, Dante is the transformation of Christian order through love, imagination, and direct access to the cosmos.

That means the same Dante cluster belongs to both spines, but with different reader questions:

- **Homer to Tolstoy:** what does Dante do to literary memory?
- **Plato to Hegel:** what does Dante do to theology, imagination, and philosophical modernity?

## Relationship To Homer To Tolstoy

The two Volume I spines answer different questions:

- [Homer to Tolstoy](homer-to-tolstoy.md): how civilization remembers
- **Plato to Hegel:** how civilization thinks

They overlap most strongly at Dante. That overlap is a feature, not a bug: it shows where literary memory and theological-philosophical order become one civilizational hinge.

## Relationship To Tolstoy

This route does not end at Tolstoy, but it prepares the Tolstoy bridge.

Kant and Hegel make it easier to understand why the later bridge into Apocalypse becomes a problem of causation, perception, history, and world-making rather than merely biography. Use [The Tolstoy Question](../../corpus/cross-volume/tolstoy-question.md) after this route when the reader is ready to cross into Volume II.

## Guardrail

Do not overstate this route into a full history of philosophy or theology. It is a public orientation spine for Predictive History's Civilization semester. It is strongest when it clarifies how sacred order, metaphysics, imagination, and historical consciousness become civilizational infrastructure.
