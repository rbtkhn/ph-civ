# ph-apo Pressure Spine

This note defines the first public organizing spine for `ph-apo`.

It is not a literary-author route like [Homer to Tolstoy](homer-to-tolstoy.md). Apocalypse is the application semester, so its equivalent spine must organize **pressure forms**, not authors.

The first public pressure spine is:

```text
Geography -> Incentives -> Causation Hinge -> Infrastructure / Sacred Systems
```

## Routed Sequence

| Step | Pressure Form | Routed Unit | Role In The Spine |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Geography | [geo-14](../cards/geo-14.md) | Chokepoints, platforms, proxies, resources, and strategic theater make escalation legible. |
| 2 | Incentives | [gt-16](../cards/gt-16.md) | Shipping, energy, deterrence, signaling, and narrative become pressure variables. |
| 3 | Causation Hinge | [sh-16](../cards/sh-16.md) | The Tolstoy-style warning enters application space: do not reduce crisis to the loudest visible actor. |
| 4 | Infrastructure / Sacred Systems | [sh-28](../cards/sh-28.md) | Finance, networks, nuclear power, sacred geography, ideology, and symbolic pressure become one contested field. |

## Why This Spine Exists

`ph-civ` teaches how historical forms are made. `ph-apo` teaches how those forms reveal themselves when systems come under pressure.

That means the reader needs an Apocalypse-facing entry that is reusable across episodes:

- geography explains constraint
- incentives explain behavior
- the causation hinge keeps analysis from collapsing into biography
- infrastructure and sacred systems explain why pressure becomes systemic rather than merely local

## Why Chronology Is Secondary

Chronology still matters in Apocalypse, but it should not be the first public organizing principle.

### What chronology is good for

- preserving archive truth and provenance
- showing how a live forecast or interpretation evolved over time
- tracing the development of one lecture series such as Geo-Strategy or Game Theory
- helping readers study a specific crisis in the order it was originally encountered

### What chronology is weak at

- teaching the reusable analytical method
- connecting structurally similar crises that happened at different moments
- preserving the bridge from Civilization, which is pattern-first rather than diary-first
- preventing the corpus from feeling like a stream of commentary instead of a pressure-reading system

The rule, therefore, is:

- **primary public organization:** pressure spine
- **secondary support organization:** chronology, provenance, and series order

## Chronology Guardrail

Do not erase chronology. Keep it available through source-video indexes, chapter lists, provenance folders, and series sequences. But do not let chronology replace the public pressure grammar of Apocalypse.

Chronology tells the reader **when** the material appeared.

The pressure spine tells the reader **why the material is analytically reusable**.
