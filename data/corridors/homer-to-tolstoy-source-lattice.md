# Homer to Tolstoy Source-Lattice

This note turns the [Homer to Tolstoy](homer-to-tolstoy.md) literary spine into
a usable source-lattice.

The governing rule is:

```text
Open the route doorway first.
Return to the routed source floor.
Use support only when the route becomes unstable.
Widen into bridge interpretation only after the literary spine is grounded.
```

That means this corridor should not be read as route prose alone. Its canonical
author sequence needs a retrieval order.

## Compact Formula

```text
doorway -> primary route floor -> secondary support -> widened crossing
```

For this corridor, that becomes:

```text
Homer-to-Tolstoy corridor -> routed chapter/card floor -> support ring ->
Tolstoy bridge into Apocalypse
```

## 1. Doorway

Open the route through the public orientation surfaces first:

- [Homer to Tolstoy](homer-to-tolstoy.md)
- [Homer to Tolstoy Support Ring](homer-to-tolstoy-support-ring.md)
- [Two Volumes, One Reader Map](../../docs/two-volumes-one-reader-map.md)

The doorway tells the reader what kind of object this is:

- the Volume I literary spine
- not a side corridor
- not a complete literary canon
- not yet a fully balanced Tolstoy shelf

## 3. Secondary Support

Only open the secondary layer when the route becomes unstable or thin.

Use it for four kinds of difficulty:

- when the reader needs pre-Homeric memory support
- when Homer needs Great Books close-read support after `civ-07`
- when the Dante middle needs strengthening
- when Shakespeare needs a stronger imperial chassis
- when the Tolstoy endpoint needs a clearer handoff into Apocalypse

### Memory and pre-literary support

## 4. Widened Crossing

Only after the route is source-grounded should it widen into higher-order
interpretation.

For this corridor, the widened layer includes:

- first-tour teaching
- literary memory arguments
- cross-volume continuity claims
- `Civilization -> Apocalypse` bridge reading
- narrative-warfare and consciousness-continuity comparisons

This is where the corridor becomes more interpretive. It should not collapse the
route prose, cards, or bridge notes into one undifferentiated authority layer.

## First-Tour Use

For first-tour use, keep the route thinner:

- open the corridor
- begin with `civ-07`
- use the canonical author sequence as the guide
- introduce support nodes only when confusion or weakness appears
- do not front-load the whole support ring

The first tour should feel like a discoverable literary spine, not an overloaded
reference apparatus.

## Reader Guardrails

- Do not treat corridor prose as a substitute for routed units.
- Do not let support-ring nodes silently become new core stops.
- Do not pretend `sh-16` is a full dedicated Tolstoy lecture.
- Do not cross into Apocalypse before the literary spine is actually grounded.
- Do not mistake widened interpretation for the same thing as the source floor.

## Why This Prototype Matters

This route is the best PH-CIV prototype for a corridor source-lattice because it
already has:

- a fixed canonical spine
- an explicit support ring
- a known weak point at the Tolstoy end
- a real cross-volume handoff into Apocalypse

That makes it a strong test case for how route architecture, source discipline,
and widening interpretation should work together in public Predictive History.
