# Homer to Tolstoy Support Ring

This note explains the support architecture around the [Homer to Tolstoy](homer-to-tolstoy.md) literary spine.

The rule is simple:

- the **core spine** stays fixed
- the **support ring** strengthens the route without changing its canonical author sequence or first-tour order

## Core Spine

The public core spine remains:

```text
Homer -> Virgil -> Dante -> Shakespeare -> Dostoevsky -> Tolstoy
```

Its primary routed units remain the ones named in the main corridor. This note does not add new core stops.

## Support Ring Rule

Use the support ring to deepen the spine in four ways:

- memory before the literary route fully emerges
- theological and scriptural reinforcement around Dante
- philosophical and imperial scaffolding around the modern turn
- a cleaner handoff from Tolstoy into the Apocalypse bridge

These support nodes strengthen the route, but they do not replace the core spine and should not be inserted into the current first-tour stop order.

## Kant In, Goethe Out For Now, Voltaire Out

Under the current **Literary Spine** rule:

- **Kant is in the support ring**
  because [civ-55](../cards/civ-55.md) already materializes him as the bridge from Dante, imagination, and love into modern philosophy.
- **Goethe is out for now**
  because the current public mirror does not yet give him a comparably strong routed placement or dedicated literary-support node.
- **Voltaire is out**
  because he belongs more naturally to Enlightenment and French-revolution context than to this particular literary-spine architecture.

This means Kant strengthens the route without becoming a new core-spine author, while Goethe and Voltaire remain contextual rather than structural.

## Tolstoy Bridge Handoff

The support ring should make the Tolstoy end of the route easier to read:

- `sh-16` completes the literary path as a routed modern coda
- [The Tolstoy Question](../../corpus/cross-volume/tolstoy-question.md) turns that coda into a causation hinge
- [Predictive History After Tolstoy](../../docs/predictive-history-after-tolstoy.md) carries the route into the Civilization-to-Apocalypse bridge

The support ring therefore strengthens the route, but does not blur the distinction between Volume I literary discovery and Volume II pressure application.

## Guardrail

Do not treat the support ring as a second canonical route. It is a strengthening architecture around the spine, not a replacement for the spine.
