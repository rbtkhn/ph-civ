# Great Books #8: The Poetry of Empire

## Where This Sits

This lecture places Virgil and empire inside the Great Books sequence, making poetry a vehicle for imperial memory, legitimacy, grief, and Roman inheritance.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a strong literary-civilizational hinge between Homeric epic and the political imagination of empire.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Poetry becomes a way empire remembers itself and justifies its losses.
- Virgil likely mediates Greek inheritance into Roman identity and imperial destiny.
- The lecture prepares the later Dante turn by showing how poetry can carry political theology.

## Limits of the Frame

This entry is in review. Claims about Virgil, empire, Roman identity, propaganda, grief, and political theology need close textual and historical review.
