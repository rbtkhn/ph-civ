# Great Books #7: The Anti-Homer

## Where This Sits

This lecture names an anti-Homeric turn, using opposition to Homer as a way to test the canon's internal argument about heroism, violence, imitation, and moral imagination.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a strong comparative hinge: the point is not only a text, but a reversal of the heroic pattern established earlier.

## Historical Pressure Points

- The chapter depends on earlier Homeric placement and asks what it means to oppose or revise Homer.
- It likely tracks anti-heroic, philosophical, or moral critique within the canon itself.
- It prepares the reader for Virgil, Dante, and later reworkings of epic inheritance.

## Limits of the Frame

This entry is in review. Anti-Homer framing, canon rivalry, moral critique, and implied textual hierarchy require later close reading and external literary review.
