# Great Books #1: Secrets of the Universe

## Where This Sits

This opening Great Books lecture establishes the course frame: reading canonical works as a way to recover full humanity, consciousness, love, imagination, and freedom from modern attention capture.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a threshold lecture and classroom provocation, not as a settled science or theology statement.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Canon reading is framed as an entrance into human interiority rather than mere content coverage.
- Kant, Plato, meditation, AI, media, school, and religious language are braided into one orientation problem.
- The lecture asks students to treat attention, imagination, and love as stakes of education.

## Limits of the Frame

This entry is in review. Consciousness, neuroscience, psychedelics, Christian language, school critique, and science-vs-spirituality claims require careful source-layering and later external review.
