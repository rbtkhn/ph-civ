# Civilization #14: Hannibal Barca, Lucius Brutus, and the Triumph of Rome

## Where This Sits

This chapter starts the Roman arc by asking why Rome, rather than the Greek cities or Carthage, becomes the next carrier of Western civilization. It places Rome inside military crisis, citizenship expansion, and republican myth.

## Reading Posture

Read it as the first Roman diagnostic chapter. The lecture is less interested in Rome as inevitable empire than in the values, manpower system, enemies, and stories that let Rome survive and absorb pressure.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Roman power is framed through manpower plus civic values.
- The Punic Wars test Rome's capacity to survive catastrophe.
- Brutus and anti-king memory become republican identity.
- Carthage's defeat creates the world that later produces Caesar.
