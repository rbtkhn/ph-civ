# Civilization #15: The Myth-Making Genius of Julius Caesar

## Where This Sits

This chapter places Caesar at the point where republican crisis becomes personal myth. Rome has defeated its enemies, but victory has created land, debt, faction, and honor pressures that Caesar can narrate around himself.

## Reading Posture

Read Caesar here as both actor and author. The lecture asks how a political figure converts campaigns, dispatches, mercy, reform, and risk into a story powerful enough to destabilize an old order.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Post-Punic expansion creates land, debt, and factional pressure.
- Caesar's commentaries make conquest part of self-authorship.
- Civil war and clemency intensify Caesar's personal centrality.
- Assassination is framed as the republic defending its old identity.
