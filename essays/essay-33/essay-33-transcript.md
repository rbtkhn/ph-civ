# World War Trump

## Part I: Full transcript

The Iranians have shot down an American F-15. Both the Americans and the Iranians are racing to find the pilot. The Iranians have damaged a Warthog as well as two Blackhawk helicopters involved in the search and rescue mission.

The stakes cannot be higher. If the Iranians capture the pilot, it will be as though they have tamed a God who had soared haughtily throwing thunderbolts. If America did not have a ground invasion plan before, it does now. Seizing Kharg Island or locating Iran’s uranium stockpiles are no longer the priority. What matters now is maintaining the illusion of America’s aerial supremacy, which Secretary of War Peter Hegseth boasted about having been achieved at the beginning of this war.

The US Air Force was not designed for a war of attrition. American airpower is designed to “shock and awe” — not “loiter over the top of Tehran” “to negotiate with bombs,” as Hegseth demanded. As this war drags on, there will be an acceleration in accidents and malfunction. To actually achieve aerial supremacy, the Americans will need to launch a ground invasion to clear Iranian air defenses. Otherwise, the Iranians will get more “lucky” in shooting down American aircrafts, and American pilots will be paraded on Iranian state television.

Is this war affecting Trump’s sleep as much as everyone else’s? Not at all. He is obsessed with turning the White House into the Mar-a-Lago of the North. He is probably more angry at a judge impeding his White House ballroom renovation than at the Iranians for defying him. Trump has even threatened to walk away — because it’s the Europeans who depend on oil from the Strait of Hormuz.

This war threatens to destroy the American empire, and collapse the global economy. Trump can sleep well at night because everything is going to plan.

In my most recent YouTube lecture, I argued that Trump’s grand plan is to use the war in the Middle East to re-structure America. Trump’s America will no longer be the world’s policeman guarding its global reserve currency status; it will become Greater North America, a self-sufficient continental fortress that sells its resources to a world in crisis.

Trump’s war aims to accomplish three objectives:

Devastate the energy infrastructure of the Middle East so that the world loses one-fifth of its energy supply.

Re-orientate the global economy to depend on the resources and manufacturing of Greater North America.

Break the political monopoly of the Military-Industrial Complex, Wall Street, and the deep state.

Last Thursday, Prime Minister Anthony Albanese, Prime Minister Keir Starmer, and President Donald Trump all addressed their respective nations. All three implied that the war has only started. We can expect frequent national addresses, as this war causes energy and food shortages across the world. JP Morgan estimates that the world will no longer have access to any Middle East energy by the end of this month.

Most came away with Trump’s speech shocked at the lack of shock. Contrary to the consensus, journalist Seymour Hersh believes that Trump used his speech to announce a ground invasion. The speech is not explicit in this regard, but Hersh is America’s investigative journalist par excellence, which has been true since he reported on the My Lai massacre. He should be taken seriously until proven otherwise.

In 2003, when most journalists and analyst believed an Iraq War was an impossibility, Hersh wrote a series of New Yorker articles quoting anonymous deep state sources that it was an inevitability. His sources were shaken and flabbergasted by how the White House was intent on war against a nation that had been hollowed out by a decade of sanctions. His sources also informed him that Vice-President Cheney and his people were hunting down leakers, and side-lining naysayers. It was essentially a soft coup.

Trump does not care for Cheney’s patience and subtlety. After Trump’s address to the nation, his Secretary of War Peter Hegseth announced the firing of three generals. Many in the military were stunned by this purge in the middle of a war when America most needed steady hands at the helm. They failed to appreciate Trump’s logic which is that war is the best opportunity to re-structure the military, and bring in loyalists.

Hegseth also announced that personnel will be allowed to carry fire-arms into military bases. He framed this new policy as empowering soldiers to defend themselves against active shooters. This fits into Hegseth’s plan to quash the woke bureaucracy, and unleash the warrior ethos — something he outlined in his speech in Quantico, Virginia last September. Those who have balls will lead, and everyone else can hide under his or her or their desk.

Meanwhile, Trump fired his Attorney General Pam Bondi, which was the first dismissal of his Second Term (Mike Walsh and Kristi Noem were re-assigned). Trump appears to find her ineffectual in persecuting his political enemies, such as James Comey. Trump aims to turn the Department of Justice into his angel of retribution.

In this post, I have made bold claims about Trump’s strategy and intention, and I make three predictions that will validate or invalidate them:

Trump intends to launch a ground invasion against Iran that will “inadvertently” destroy the energy infrastructure of the GCC. This means that Trump wants a long drawn-out war that destroys Iran’s critical civilian infrastructure, provoking Iran to retaliate against the GCC. This explains why Trump blew up a bridge in Iran, and has threatened to destroy Iran’s power plants.

Trump will not fire Tulsi Gabbard, and she and JD Vance will be made more prominent as this war drags on. (There were rumors that Trump’s advisors pressured him to fire Gabbard, who was the most skeptical of this war.) Trump cannot stop this war from escalating, as the Pentagon would not stomach a humiliating retreat. But he can absolve himself of responsibility for this war, and that means putting the blame on Jared Kushner and Peter Hegseth while aligning himself more with Vance and Gabbard.

At some point, Trump will shift his attention to creating Greater North America. He may seek to annex Cuba, and he may seek to strike the Mexican cartels. He will encourage Alberta’s bid to secede from Canada — a movement that is picking up steam.

It is hard to believe that Trump can be so diabolical, but the greatest trick the devil played was to make us believe he was incapable of trickery. Think of Captain Jack Sparrow whose clumsinesss is so outrageous that it is elegant. That in a nutshell is Trump, whose stupidity is so flagrant that it is brilliant.

Trump’s stupidity has mesmerized his opponents into paralysis, and the Democrats believe that just by waiting Trump out they can reclaim the White House in 2028. Trump’s enemies find him so contemptuous that they can only see madness, and never method.

Consider Trump sending in ground troops to control the Strait of Hormuz. Everyone scoffs at Trump because this may provoke the Iranians into closing the Strait to all traffic — but what if that is Trump’s true intention?

There is one last benefit to Trump’s stupidity, and it is that it gives him maximum flexibility. Having failed to articulate a reason and a strategy for this war, Trump is not bounded by any reason or strategy. This war may have provoked a MAGA civil war, but if Trump were to announce he’s ending the war to focus on crushing the deep state then MAGA would rally behind him.

Trump has turned stupidity into his genius.
