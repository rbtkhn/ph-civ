# Carney’s Speech and Trump’s Letter

## Part I: Full transcript

In this year’s meeting of the global elite in Davos, Switzerland, the Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney received a standing ovation. He is only the third man to have received such a distinction from such a somber audience (the other two were Nelson Mandela and Volodymyr Zelenskyy). His speech would have won a freshman composition award at Harvard or Oxford, and Carney had studied at both.

Carney invoked two allusions. The first was to Thucydides, whose The Peloponnesian War illustrated how the Athenian empire cannibalized its allies. Carney implored the world to stand together against such mafia imperialism. “If you’re not at the table, you’re on the menu” were his memorable choice of words.

Carney also invoked Vaclav Havel’s essay “The Power of the Powerless,” which argued that tyranny is based on mass hypnosis and compliance. He said that the international rules-based order was always a myth that benefited the American empire – and America’s allies went along with its War on Terror, its economic strangling of “rogue regimes,” and its enabling of the Israeli genocide in Gaza because they too benefited. But now that Trump is threatening to annex Greenland and Canada, we must speak truth to power, and build a true international rules-based order.

Carney’s speech was well-received because it had the accent and attitude of a leader molded in seminars in the Ivy League and summers in the Hamptons. It contrasts starkly with Trump’s letter to the Norwegians, in which he threatened to annex Greenland in retribution for being denied the Nobel Peace Prize:

“Considering your Country decided not to give me the Nobel Peace Prize for having stopped 8 Wars PLUS, I no longer feel an obligation to think purely of Peace, although it will always be predominant, but can now think about what is good and proper for the United States of America.”

The letter was so raw and egotistical that it’s surprising Trump did not speak truth to power: “How dare you give the Nobel Peace Prize to Obama, and not give it to me, you racist pricks!”

Carney left Davos without meeting Trump, but Trump and his minions did name-check and insult Carney. This feud will intensify, and it will lead to a majority government for Carney, and divert the public gaze from the asset stripping of Canada, which is his true agenda. He will introduce digitial ID and digital currency, and sell Alberta to the Americans, and everywhere else to the Chinese and Qataris. Canadians are so blinded by their TDS that they will cheer for their own enslavement. Carney will finish his career groveling before Trump at Mar-a-Lago before retiring to an English manor.

This may all seem like theater in the end, but Carney and Trump truly loathe each other. Carney is the consummate insider who became the Bank of England’s first foreign Governor. He was happiest when he could be Justin Trudeau’s puppet-master, but when Trudeau proved too stupid even for the Canadians he was forced onto the stage. Cartoonishly evil at times, he is an eminence grise who basks in the shadows, and whose soul screams in horror at the light.

Trump is the typical parvenu, and the New York elite have always mocked him as a reality television clown even before the advent of reality television (it was called the tabloids back in the day). When Trump found himself in the White House, the cozy club of Washington DC conspired to humiliate and persecute him. At the United Nations, the Germans laughed at Trump when he mentioned the Nordstream pipeline and Germany’s energy reliance on Russia. Pelosi clapped condescendingly at Trump, and Fauci rolled his eyes. His subordinates bragged about disobeying him, and America’s top military officer Mark Milley called the Chinese to tell them to ignore Trump.

But Trump had been mocked at all his life, and he responded by creating his own world. When the social elite of Palm Beach refused Trump’s entry into their clubs, Trump started Mar-a-Lago, which he has turned into the center of his universe. Trump’s Board of Peace promises to be the geo-political equivalent of Mar-a-Lago.

Mark my words: Trump is intent on vengeance. He has convinced himself that Obama and the European elite stole the 2020 election, that the deep state manufactured the January 6th riots, and that the Democrats bankrupted him with lawfare. Like a man bullied too much in high school, his final will is to hunt down and destroy his enemies.

Take Greenland, for example. Does anyone seriously believe that Trump wants to annex Greenland for geo-political, strategic, and economic reasons? He aims to humiliate the European elite, who worship Obama as much as they loathe Trump. He wants to show that they have no clothes, and ignite a mass revolt.

Yes, the Europeans have convinced Trump to a compromise, but that’s only because Trump hates private conflict as much as he relishes public spats. It is funny to Trump that after Carney urged the world speak truth to power, the Europeans privately begged him to maintain the illusion that they are allies and not vassals. In a month or so, Trump will return to threatening Greenland.

As the crowned prince of the Davos crowd, Carney appreciates Trump’s nature in a way that Trump himself does not. But like the rest of the Davos class, Carney lives in a bubble so tight that he cannot hear himself speak.

Refuse to comply, and speak truth to power? Carney is the leader of an authoritarian regime that is working hard to ban guns and free speech. In 2022, Ottawa de-banked anyone who donated a dollar to the trucker protestors, accusing them of sedition. Carney seeks to build a grand coalition of the free with China and Qatar. Somehow a badly-written Saturday Night Live sketch – in which a minion of the high school bully runs to the teacher once the bully turns on him – has turned into a rallying cry for the global financial elite.

I forced myself to listen to a few of this year’s Davos panel and speeches. These men and women – such as Larry Fink, Jamie Dimon, Christine Lagarde – collectively rule the world, and I was struck by how vacuous, self-indulgent, and tired they all seem to be. We know that our world is ending because evil has ceased to be vile, and has merely become pathetic.
