# Secret History #8: The Rise of the American Kakistocracy

## Part I: Full transcript

I think it is fair to say that everything sucks now. For all his faults, Steve Jobs was obsessed with creating a superior product. He would manipulate and bully his team until they met his high expectations, and they loved him for it. In his biography of Jobs, Walter Isaacson said that it was as though Jobs had a “reality distortion projector.”

When Tim Cook took over Apple in August 2011, the company was valued at $350 billion. Today, it is worth almost four trillion dollars.

Predictive History Substack is a reader-supported publication. To receive new posts and support my work, consider becoming a free or paid subscriber.

Jobs would have been appalled. He was focused on putting out innovative products that consumers fetishized. Apple has not introduced an innovative product since 2015.

Warren Buffett once advised Jobs on how to make his company more valuable. Buffett suggested stock buybacks, and Jobs responded that that sort of financial shenanigans would destroy his company. When Tim Cook embraced Buffett’s strategy, he proved both right. Under Cook’s leadership, Apple has spent over 700 billion in buying back stocks. Berkshire Hathaway has made over $100 billion from its Apple investments.

Cook is lauded as a financial genius, but all he did was ride the coattails of Jobs’ legacy, and cannibalize Apple for stockholders – with the same speed and ferocity as Jack Welch when he hollowed out General Electric. Cook’s replacement will take the corpse he inherits, and butcher it to sell piecemeal – the standard private equity playbook.

It feels like the end of days, and everyone wants to cash out. I worked long and hard to build up my Predictive History YouTube channel. I permitted people to use my content, hoping that they would add value to it (doing translations, simplifying and expanding content, and adding in a video montage). I want to shout out Bede Briscomb for doing exactly that. Unfortunately, there are bad faith actors on the Internet who take advantage of my openness and generosity to charge monthly YouTube memberships. Too few want to create value, and too many want to appropriate the hard work of others.

I do not agree in any way with Charlie Kirk’s politics, but I respect the man’s ground game. He was passionate and dedicated, and that’s why he won so many converts.

Kirk’s replacement Brilyn Hollyhand has become the anti-Kirk, and he’s destroying Kirk’s legacy one campus at a time. That’s the point. Turning Point USA’s donors used Kirk to build it from the ground up, and they’ll use Hollyhand to burn it into ashes. What matters is that they and only they are in control. Obey, or perish.

That is the message that the Ellisons have sent to CBS News with their appointment of Bari Weiss as the editor-in-chief. This Netanyahu toady became infamous for calling Tulsi Gabbard an Assad toady without knowing what “toady” meant. Her handlers had given her certain talking points for her Joe Rogan appearance, and she could not be bothered to do her homework. Weiss is too in love with her own mediocrity to possess any self-doubt.

Witness the letter that Weiss sent to the staff of CBS News. In it, she outlined ten core journalistic values:

1. Journalism that reports on the world as it actually is.

2. Journalism that is fair, fearless and factual.

3. Journalism that respects our audience enough to tell the truth plainly – wherever it leads.

4. Journalism that makes sense of a noisy, confusing world.

5. Journalism that explains things clearly, without pretension or jargon.

6. Journalism that holds both American political parties to equal scrutiny.

7. Journalism that embraces a wide spectrum of views and voices so that the audience can contend with the best arguments on all sides of a debate.

8. Journalism that rushes toward the most interesting and important stories, regardless of their unpopularity.

9. Journalism that uses all of the tools of the digital era.

10. Journalism that understands that the best way to serve America is to endeavor to present the public with the facts, first and foremost.

Let’s give Bari Weiss some credit here. This writing would not pass a high school English class in China, but at least she refused to use AI. No AI model could possibly produce such garbage. She wrote it while drunk on her own narcissism. Perhaps she was too devoted to smearing anti-Zionist professors while at Columbia to bother to learn how to write without repetition and redundancy, which would serve the public interest first and foremost in a way that is most interesting and important.

Another individual who has failed upwardly to the sun, stars, and moon is Sam Altman. Yes, he is lame, but he is honest. He warns us that AI can destroy the world, and that is happening. As the journalist Karen Hao explains in her new book, billions have been poured into ChatGPT and other AI models, but no one has figured out how to make money. It is a trillion-dollar bubble waiting to pop, and when it does it will crater the American economy. Then there is how AI is destroying the planet with its insatiable thirst for freshwater and electricity.

It is perhaps not technologically feasible for AI to achieve “super-intelligence,” but given the rapid decline of civilization it may soon converge with humanity. Hollywood was always crappy, but now and then it offered glimpses of hope with movies such as Kubrick’s Dr. Strangelove, Scorsese’s Raging Bull, and Lynch’s Mulholland Drive. During the tyrannical reign of the Marvel Cinematic Universe, I learned to enjoy the movies by only watching them on long flights. Now I would have to shoot a bullet into my forehead to suffer today’s movies.

Welcome to the American kakistocracy, a time when a woman who calls for the overthrow of the government of Venezuela can be awarded the Nobel Peace Prize.

I’d like to hide myself under a blanket now. Please wake me up when the nukes start flying. I’d like to see there’s hope for humanity, after all.
