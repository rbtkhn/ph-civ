# The Secret History of the World #6: Manchurian Candidates

## Part I: Full transcript

Like most people, I first heard of JD Vance when I read his memoir *Hillbilly Elegy*, which I liked. I was struck by how similar it was to Barack Obama’s memoir *Dreams From My Father*. Their difference was white and black, but both never knew their fathers, both had undependable demanding mothers, and both were saved by loving grandparents. Both believed they were products of their racial environments, and both sought to save America by transcending their troubled childhoods.

As someone who has read thousands of books, I noticed artificialities in both books, as well as a tone and a personality carefully constructed for political purposes. Both books seemed to have been written by committee. I also saw at the core of both memoirs a schizophrenic and shattered soul. These similarities convinced me that JD Vance would one day attempt to become President of the United States.

We know what a Vance Presidency would look like because we experienced an Obama one. After the Great Financial Crisis, Obama rode a wave of discontent into the Presidency with his promise of “hope and change.” He won the Nobel Peace Prize for being the first Black President, and he appointed Larry Summers and Timothy Geithner, who promptly bailed out their Wall Street friends. The Obama presidency was the golden years for coastal liberal elites who wanted to feel good about themselves. It was they who prospered, and it was Blacks who suffered the most:

https://www.peoplespolicyproject.org/project/destruction-of-black-wealth-during-the-obama-presidency/

Watch Obama’s speeches, and you will see in hindsight that he is a hollow man, who thunders with eloquence and without conviction. Any body language expert can tell you that Obama seemed much happier getting out of the White House than Trump seemed getting into it. After Obama left the White House, he only deigned to re-emerge to sabotage Bernie Sanders in 2020, and to scold black men in 2024:

https://www.npr.org/2024/10/10/g-s1-27633/barack-obama-kamala-harris-black-men-pennsylvania

No one has accused Obama of being a Manchurian Candidate, but he sure acts and behaves like one.

It would be unfair to single out Barack Obama for being a Manchurian Candidate when the upper echelons of Western politics are over-brimming with them. Candace Owens reminds us that while it’s fun to speculate on the gender of Brigitte Macron, the real issue is that somehow a mediocre Emmanuel Macron (his schoolmates remember him as remarkably stupid) glided into the French Presidency. The same is true for Justin Trudeau in Canada, and George W. Bush in the United States.

Of all these Manchurian Candidates, we should have the most sympathy for George W. Bush, who in his eager but incompetent manner rebelled against his father and the other monsters who have destroyed the world. In a speech condemning Putin’s invasion of Ukraine, he highlighted the death of Russia’s democratic institutions as the main cause. “The result is an absence of checks and balances in Russia and the decision of one man to launch a wholly unjustified and brutal invasion of Iraq,” he said, before catching himself. He corrected himself, and said “I meant Ukraine.” Then he decided to double down, and mutter, “Iraq, too.” The audience laughed, and Bush shrugged his shoulders, and said, “I’m 75.”

What are we to make of this? We can interpret his “I’m 75” statement to mean that he no longer has control of his words (but did he ever?). We can also interpret it to mean that he’s too old to care. It’s understood that when he said “the decision of one man to launch a wholly unjustified and brutal invasion of Iraq” he was referring to himself, but he said it in a manner that was “dissociative.” He could have been referring to his father, to Dick Cheney, or to himself who was not himself. It was probably all three.

In his mind he might have been thinking: “I am sick of this, and I don’t want to pretend anymore. My father is dead, and the world he loved is evil and dying. Everyone knows we did 9/11, as well as the Maidan Coup in 2014, which is what provoked Putin’s invasion. Everyone knows I’m a puppet, and now that the puppet-master has died it’s time to tell the truth.”

George W. Bush was fortunate enough to be born into a large powerful family, and so his “programming” was not so complete as to have extinguished his soul. We cannot say the same for Obama, and certainly not for Vance. Vance has already sold his soul to the Catholic Church and Peter Thiel.

Of all the Manchurian Candidates, Vance may prove to be the most volatile and dangerous. He is a puppet in a time when nearly all the grand puppet-masters have died out. It’s time for the puppets to come out and play.
