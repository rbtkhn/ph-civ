# Vietnam Redux

## Part I: Full transcript

Two weeks into this war, and it is going as well as anyone who is outside Washington could expect. The United States and Israel continue to attempt to bomb Iran into submission, which like most aerial bombardment campaigns has hardened the resolve of the Iranian people. There have been notable successes, such as the assassination of the Ayatollah Khamenei on the first day. There have also been inexcusable tragedies, such as the bombing of a girls’ school in southern Iran that killed over 170, also on the first day.

Iran’s fast and furious response — closing the Strait of Hormuz, targeting US military assets throughout the Middle East, and pressuring the GCC economies — has surprised and shaken the Trump administration. In a major escalation, Trump has ordered the bombing of Kharg Island, and the Pentagon has ordered the deployment of at least 2,500 marines to the Middle East from Japan. There is now fear that the marines will be ordered to seize and occupy Kharg Island, which Trump believes would strangle the Iranian war economy.

Kharg Island prepares ninety percent of Iran’s oil for export, primarily to China. The Iranian coastline is too shallow for large oil tankers, forcing Iran to rely on Kharg Island and its deep waters.

Trump’s calculus may be that if he were to cut out the economic heart of the Islamic Republic then Iran could no longer build drones, pay its soldiers, and purchase Russian and Chinese support. The economy would collapse, the citizens would revolt, and the security forces would wither away. In 1988, Trump (then a private citizen) fantasized about seizing Kharg Island:

“I’d be harsh on Iran. They’ve been beating us psychologically, making us look like a bunch of fools. One bullet shot at one of our men or ships, and I’d do a number on Kharg Island. I’d go in and take it.”

Hopefully, this is yet another of Trump’s hare-brained schemes that amount to nothing, such as his plan to bribe the Kurds into the war. Let’s look at why such a strategy would fail:

During the Iran-Iraq War, Kharg Island was bombed continuously. Iran found workarounds, such as loading the oil onto smaller ships by the coast, and then transferring it to bigger ships in deeper waters.

Iran has stated that it aims to push oil to two hundred dollars a barrel, which would cripple the global economy. By striking at the heart of the Iranian oil economy, Trump would give Iran the justification it needs to attack GCC oil refineries. The Saudi energy infrastructure is especially vulnerable to Houthi drone and missile attacks. The Houthis could also close down Bab el Mandeb.

Control over trade access is a more valuable commodity than oil exports, and Iran can now impose tariffs strategically. Iran permits China safe passage, and promises that to any GCC nations that expel their American envoys. After negotiations, Iran permitted two Indian oil tankers to pass through the Strait of Hormuz.

Russia and China will not abandon Iran just because it can no longer export oil. As long as the Iranians prove that they are willing to fight, Russia and China will provide financing. Senator Lindsay Graham famously said that financing the war in Ukraine to kill Russians was “the best money US has ever spent.” Now that Trump is forced to remove oil sanctions against Russian oil, Putin is happy to use his oil riches to pay back the Americans for Ukraine.

There are rumors that America will use the 9th Marine Expeditionary Brigade to seize and occupy Kharg Island. (Coincidentally, this was the same brigade that on March 8, 1965 landed in Da Nang, which marked the beginning of a full-scale American ground involvement in Vietnam.)

Having thousands of American marines exposed to Iranian artillery and drone attacks would justify a much larger American invasion to control the Iranian coastline. Of course, these tens of thousands of American soldiers would then be exposed to attacks from the Zagros mountains. This would then demand hundreds of thousands of American soldiers to hunt down and destroy Iran’s underground missile cities.

From a military perspective, this is a fool’s errand. But this entire war has been one since the start. Everyone knew that at the onset of war Iran would immediately close the Strait of Hormuz — except the Trump administration.

America is an empire in decline, and suffers from a toxic combination of hubris and desperation — it can no longer behave strategically.

Consider the war in Ukraine, which has proven to be an endless series of self-defeating gambles. Blowing up the Nordstream pipeline damaged the German economy much more so than the Russian one. By freezing three hundred billion dollars in Russian assets, the Americans and Europeans nuked the credibility of their financial systems — the best money Putin ever spent. For whatever reason, Ukraine launched one failed counter-offensive after another against a well-entrenched enemy with lethal expertise in artillery and drone warfare. In December 2025, a drone strike at one of Putin’s residences scuttled any peace possibilities.

Let us recall how the Americans found themselves in this quagmire in Iran. Last June, in the 12-Day War, Israel launched a series of successful decapitation strikes before Iran struck back hard at Tel Aviv, forcing the American off-ramp. In January, Israel fomented nation-wide protests in Iran, but the Iranians cracked down before the Americans could intervene with air support. In both instances, the Americans and Israelis believed that Tehran was a cowardly and corrupt house of cards, and so they could achieve regime change cheaply, easily, and quickly. The Trump administration was not prepared for Iran’s fast and furious response because Iran had failed to respond to any past provocations (starting with the assassination of General Qasem Soleimani in January 2020). The Iranians had lulled the Americans into complacency with their “rope a dope” strategy.

The last thing that the Trump administration would do is admit the Iranians had out-smarted them, and retreat from the Middle East. Recently, Trump blamed Jared Kusher, Steve Witkoff, and Peter Hegseth for tricking him into this war. With the bombing of Kharg Island and the marine deployment, Trump signals he intends to double down rather than back down.

Trump is looking to throw a Hail Mary pass. If the Iranians refused to surrender when their Supreme Leader was assassinated, perhaps they will once Kharg Island is taken? Once you deploy boots on the ground, mission creep and sunk cost fallacy kick in, and soon enough Iran becomes another Vietnam.

An American ground invasion would necessitate a national draft, and there are signs. In 2024, Congress passed a law to automatically register fighting-age males for a national draft. The Trump officials who would oversee a draft — including Pam Bondi, Stephen Miller, Peter Hegseth, Marco Rubio, and Kristi Noem — have moved into military bases. The Trump administration has plans to deploy the National Guard across the nation in case of unrest.

In a recent interview in the White House, Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent was called away to an urgent meeting with Trump, and when he returned he looked shaken, and stammered out the following words:

“Uh, the Iranian mission is proceeding well ahead of schedule. You know, I have to tell you, Wil, that I’ve a teenage– teenager who’s considering military service. And I could give this team my highest compliment from President Trump to the head of the Joint Chiefs, to the Secretary of War. I would– they [incomprehensible] that I would trust my child’s life in their hands.”

This scene was scripted to generate online discussion to prepare young Americans mentally for a national draft.

The President who promised America that he would end all wars has started the war that may end America.
