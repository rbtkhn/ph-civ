# Secret History #9: The War of Heaven and Hell

## Part I: Full transcript

In his Divine Comedy, Dante ascends to the Heavens to meet God, and to do what God cannot do. He must imagine the Truth, and tell the world this Truth. He would spend twenty years wrestling with the blinding illumination that is God, and from this struggle he would imagine the nature of God and the universe, and our place and role in it.

The contradiction within the universe is that God is perfect and omniscient but God seeks newness and to know. To resolve this conundrum, we were created with corporeal bodies – to suffer and to blunder so that we could experience and imagine new possibilities. God granted us the greatest gift – free will – so that our imaginations may roam freely.

But how could we know good and evil? God breathed the divine spark into Adam, and so this divine spark is part of our nature. This essence of God – what Dante refers to as “Love” – seeks to return to Itself. It was his eternal love for Beatrice that brought Dante to Heaven.

When we love, the spark grows in us, connecting us to the ultimate Good, and igniting our imagination. When we despise, the spark dims in us, and we become removed from our true nature.

Dante knew that the universe is split into infinite dimensions, and the higher dimensions are of spirit, and the lower are of matter. Dante depicts Lucifer as a soulless and thoughtless machine, mechanically flapping its wings to cool the fire, excrement, and screams of Hell.

If Dante walked amongst us today, he would exclaim that we have engineered a Hell out of Heaven. He would tell us that Lucifer walks among us today, and it feeds on our souls. We have surrendered our free will, and we worship a false idol.

Dante would see Lucifer manifest itself in every aspect of our lives. It is the worship of money, and the belief that consumerism is the ultimate freedom when it is the final slavery. It is the devotion to the Internet, and the delusion that technology can liberate all when it has imprisoned all. It is the blind faith in the individual, and the fallacy that neo-liberalism offers meaning when it seeks to consume all. Dante would agree with Alexander Dugin and the Eastern Orthodox tradition that Western civilization itself is the Anti-Christ.

Ever since I became suddenly famous in June, I have preached nothing but destruction and death. Many students and viewers have asked: What’s the point of living in such hopeless times?

My answer is this: Living has the most meaning in the most hopeless of times.

Let us embrace the Divine by invoking the confidence and spirit of Dante. Without any notable achievements, Dante was certain that he would create the greatest achievement. He wrote an epic in which the laws of the universe were split apart to pave his ascent to Heaven. The angels there saw Dante’s imagination as the animating force of the universe, which would heal all wounds and fractures.

What does it mean to be Divine nowadays? It means to be human because the Divine is within each of us.

In her book The Human Condition, Hannah Arendt explains that there are two actions that define the Divine in us, and which sets right the universe. The first is the act of making promises, and the second is the act of forgiving others.

We see this idea manifest itself in Homer’s Iliad. As he is about to sail to Troy in search of glory, Achilles promises the father of Patroclus that he will always protect his son. When Achilles gets into a bitter feud with Agamemnon, Patroclus becomes a pawn. Achilles refuses to fight until Agamemnon apologizes, and Agamemnon would rather see all the Greeks die. As Hector and the Trojans are about to burn the Greek ships, Patroclus leaps into the chasm, and pretends to be Achilles to rally the Greeks, only to be brought low by Hector. In his murderous rage, Achilles slays Hector, and mutilates his body to the disgust of the Greeks, the Trojans, and the Gods. Achilles falls into a deep depression, and nothing sates his sleepless numbness. In his self-hatred, Achilles mutilates Hector’s body from dawn until dusk.

The Gods bring Hector’s father Priam into Achilles’ tent. He has seen Achilles mutilate his beloved son, and he wants to stab him in the throat. Instead, he kneels down, and kisses the hand of his Satan. Achilles is shocked into submission by Priam’s courage and love for Hector, and at that moment all the hatred and anger in the universe dissipates.

Christians believe that Jesus came to forgive humanity for the Original Sin, and Rudolf Steiner promises that there will be a Second Coming. In an age of tumult and tribulation, we will learn to forgive ourselves and each other so that we can promise each other a new future. Steiner calls this the “Christ Consciousness.” Once we become the “Christ Consciousness” we bring forth the Messianic Age.

I recently had tea with a Vedic monk who told me all of humanity’s greatest souls are clamoring to reincarnate into our time. It is in this age of darkness that we can best achieve enlightenment.

The War of Heaven and Hell is not fought between nation-states. It is fought everyday within our own souls. The universe is in us, and the universe reflects us. As above so below.

Let us not despair then. Let us feel blessed, for the Messianic Age is about to dawn on us.

Dante and Homer can light the path, but we must have the love and courage to walk it ourselves.
