# Iran, Greenland, and Canada

## Part I: Full transcript

I am experimenting with a new format this week. I will examine three major geo-political events of the past week, and provide analysis and context.

Let’s start with Iran. Many expected America to strike Iran, but Trump canceled airstrikes. There’s reporting that Qatar, Turkey, and Saudi Arabia talked Trump out of bombing Iran because they were afraid of regional instability. To maintain the narrative that Israel is in charge, Netanyahu claimed that he asked Trump to call off the strikes. What happened?

Let’s look at the chain of events that led us to this point. Netanyahu visited Trump at Mar-a-Lago to celebrate New Year’s. Currency speculators caused the Iranian Rial to collapse, leading to protests from merchants disgusted with corruption and mismanagement. These protests spread quickly, and promised to engulf the nation. Mike Pompeo tweeted a New Year’s greeting to the Iranian protestors and “to every Mossad agent walking beside them.” Trump warned that the United States would protect protestors, some of whom were targeting policemen.

Then, possibly with technology provided by the Russians or the Chinese, the Iranians jammed the Starlink terminals that permitted co-ordination and communication among the protestors; the same technology enabled them to track down Mossad assets. Turkey relayed intelligence that allowed the Iranians to wipe out Kurdish insurgents who crossed the border from Iraq. Rumors circulated on social media that the Iranians have arrested possible collaborators, including a former President as well as Foreign Minister. The security services remained loyal.

Israel and the United States deployed the standard color revolution playbook in Iran, but just like in the 12-day war they underestimated the regime’s resolve and resources. Trump had to call off airstrikes because Iran had stamped out the protests. In the playbook, airstrikes are like thunder and lightning from the Gods — which would drum up the resolve of protestors, sap the morale of the security forces, and sway public opinion. By themselves, the airstrikes would accomplish nothing. Israel and Trump will now have to manufacture another pretext, which means that we’ll see a regional false flag attack.

Let’s move on to Greenland, which has caused a major rift in NATO. Trump seems determined to annex Greenland, and the Europeans responded with an overwhelming display of their military might. Germany sent fifteen soldiers, and France sent thirteen to defend Greenland. This begs the question: Will Germany and France have anyone to defend the home-front against Putin? To bolster public confidence, Britain announced that it will now require 65-year olds to fight.

This Greenland thing is a farce. Trump will get Greenland, and there’s nothing the Europeans can do about it. He hates the Europeans, and he enjoys trolling them. But even Trump can’t out-do the Europeans in trolling themselves.

Let’s conclude with Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney’s recent trip to China, in which he declared a “New World Order.” Many Canadians fear that Carney’s hatred of Trump will permit the Dragon to swallow the Beaver whole.

As a former Governor of the Bank of England, Carney is first and foremost an agent of transnational capital who needs to dump Canada before it implodes. Canadian households are among the most indebted in the world, and the Canadian housing market (which is forty percent of its GDP) is facing long-term decline because Canadians only want to flip houses, and want to kick out immigrants who flip burgers.

What does an investment banker (Carney used to work for Goldman Sachs) do with a toxic asset? He dumps it on foreign investors. In the short-term, Carney is happy to sell canola to the Chinese. In the long-term, he wants Chinese to buy up overpriced crappy houses, enroll in diploma mills, and invest in Canada’s resources.

Trump wants China to prop up the ponzi scheme that is the US dollar. Chinese households have a forty percent savings rate, the highest in the world. OECD countries on average have a household savings rate of 6-10 percent. What accounts for the difference? Chinese are pessimistic by nature, and given the grim geo-political outlook they are wise to be.

But Chinese being Chinese, they are most pessimistic about themselves. That’s why they would convert their savings from yuan to USD, if the government were to permit this. This is what Trump wants to negotiate with China in April. In this way, Carney’s trip to Beijing was not to pivot Canada away from Trump — it was to test the waters for him.
