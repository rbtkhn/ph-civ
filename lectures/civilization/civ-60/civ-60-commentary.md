## Layer 1 - Neutral Summary
The lecture opens with Bretton Woods and the postwar financial order. Jiang frames the dollar, central banks, the Bank for International Settlements, the World Bank, the IMF, the Bank of England, the Federal Reserve, and Carroll Quigley as the institutional core of American imperial power.

The middle sections describe Pax Americana as a shift from civilization and nation-state sacrifice into a game of consumerism, financialization, debt, easy money, and central-bank control. Japan, the Plaza Accord, the Asian financial crisis, the end of the Soviet Union, 9/11, the war on terror, the 2008 crisis, and China are treated as stages in the system's expansion and fragility.

The final sections move into Russia, Ukraine, resources, Iran, Israel, Trump, China, NATO, the Peloponnesian War analogy, and the claim that American imperial decline may produce a major conflict. The lecture ends by returning to Homer, Dante, Kant, imagination, love, and the human task of preserving humanity in dark times.
