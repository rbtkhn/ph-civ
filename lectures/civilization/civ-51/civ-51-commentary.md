## Layer 1 - Neutral Summary

The lecture begins with a comparative map of four modern civilizations: Russian, German, British, and American. It then narrows to Britain and asks how Shakespeare transforms English into a language of empire and a carrier of Anglo-American culture.

Jiang presents Shakespeare as a poet-theatrical force who changes imagination through diction, rhythm, imagery, theater, and psychological characterization. The lecture treats language as soft power and art as a form of civilizational rewiring.

The chapter moves through high-risk claims about empire, race, white man's burden, Othello, cultural superiority, and modern Anglo-American identity. These claims need careful separation between lecture representation, literary interpretation, imperial ideology, and external historical review.
