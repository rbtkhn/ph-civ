## Layer 1 - Neutral Summary

The lecture asks how England became the greatest empire and answers through geography, repeated invasion, open competition, and adaptive institutions. Britain is framed as an island fortress whose vulnerability and protection together produce a distinctive imperial pathway.

Jiang connects British development to naval power, constitutional settlement, Protestant strands, and the later American inheritance. The chapter treats Britain less as a static nation than as a system that learns from pressure and turns maritime position into global reach.

The ending uses America and contemporary political analogies to show British inheritance continuing in contested form. Those claims are important to the course arc but must be treated as live-current and interpretive rather than settled historical judgment.
