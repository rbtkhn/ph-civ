## Layer 1 - Neutral Summary

The lecture opens by treating the Protestant Reformation as one of the hardest and most synthetic chapters in the course: a religious rupture that gives birth to capitalism. Jiang begins by contrasting Catholic hierarchy, orthodoxy, and works with Protestant direct access to God and Scripture.

The middle of the chapter argues that Protestant access creates anxiety. If each believer must prove or realize true faith without the church as mediator, work, discipline, money-making, and signs of election become psychologically charged. Jiang links this anxiety to capitalism.

The ending moves through Weber and Durkheim. Capitalism is treated as a belief system fed by anxiety, while suicide and disconnection become signs of capitalism's human cost. The lecture closes by warning that theocratic reaction may appear as a response to capitalist disconnection.
