# Interviews #11: Tucker Carlson — Iran War, Energy, and Global Order

## Core Strategic Thesis

The interview extends Predictive History themes into long-form Q&A: the Iran war as attrition (parallel to Ukraine), energy and GCC / petrodollar coupling to US fiscal position, East Asia (Japan resilience vs China “middle kingdom,” Korea’s exposure), Greater Israel as game-theoretic beneficiary if US retrenchment, and a closing arc on Western civilization vs institutional hostility to classics in elite universities and English-speaking societies.

## Defined Terms (speaker usage)

- War of attrition — Neither side concedes; long runway; global energy and food stress.
- Petrodollar / GCC recycling — Gulf sells oil in USD and recycles into US assets; loss of US security umbrella risks chain exit.
- Greater Israel project — Territorial and eschatological frame from Nile to Euphrates; US seen as constraint and enabler.
- Eschatological fever — Israel and US evangelical drivers; Third Temple / Al-Aqsa motifs in dialogue.

## Claims, Forecasts, And Falsifiers

1. Iran conflict will resemble Ukraine: prolonged attrition, hard ceasefire, Larajani-style off-ramp removal tightens escalation.
2. US lacks a cheap off-ramp with Iran without Middle East withdrawal and reparations-scale demands; GCC alignment and dollar role bind Washington.
3. Three trends: de-industrialization, remilitarization (Japan, others), mercantilism / self-sufficient supply chains.
4. Japan vs China bet: cultural resilience and history cited; Korea chaebol / exam culture tied to fertility collapse.
5. Israel benefits if US limits are exposed; Russia benefits via oil sanctions relief and war economy linkage.
6. Trump framed as unknowable mix: actor, messianic calling, Netanyahu pressure, or compromise — speculation explicitly bounded.
7. Closing: Canada, UK, Western Europe named as most hostile to Western civilization in practice; China promotes classics in Jiang’s pedagogy narrative.
