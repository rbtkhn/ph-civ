# Interviews #2: Glenn Diesen — The West's Civilizational Collapse

## Core Strategic Thesis

Long-form civilizational interview: three factors (energy, openness, cohesion) as rise/decline lens; Western / Anglo-American decline across those axes; sociological and macro theories (Turchin elite overproduction, Piketty rentier / r > g, Spengler organic lifecycles); 2008 as turning point and financialization / elite capture narrative; Roman Gracchi / Caesar as populism precedent; Trump as disruption / agency frame; Ukraine war interpretation (Russian initiative vs NATO / Kiev constraints); US–China as interdependent status quo with rapprochement possible; skepticism on durability of Russia–China–Iran alignment; closing watch on “Pax Judea” / capital allocation thesis vs domestic Israeli strain (host pushback).

## Defined Terms (speaker usage)

- Energy / openness / cohesion — Bundle for civilizational vitality (rise) and decay (decline).
- Pax Judea — Speaker frame for Israel / regional / capital projection (distinct from “nation of Israel” in closing exchange).

## Claims, Forecasts, And Falsifiers

1. Great powers and “new empire” candidates map onto the three factors (including Pax Judea as named thesis).
2. The West scores poorly on energy (labor will), openness (speech / campus clamps), and cohesion (polarization, immigration stress) — rapid decline horizon (5–10 years) as stated.
3. Collapse vs decline : elite overproduction, financialization, and Spenglerian cycle leave few peaceful exits (as argued).
4. 2008 : neoliberal / Reagan-era trends culminate ; Obama-era QE / bailouts reinforce Wall Street / elite alignment (speaker claim).
5. Ukraine : war “over” militarily in effect ; Russian adaptation and morale vs Ukrainian constraints ; only military resolution ; Odessa as strategic closure scenario (speaker).
6. China is a status quo power benefiting from globalization ; US–China may trend toward rapprochement (speaker).
7. Russia–China–Iran bloc faces historical / geographic friction (e.g. Kazakhstan / routes / currency leadership).
8. US forward deployment in Asia can be stabilizing for China relative to regional rival dynamics (Japan, India, etc. — speaker argument).
9. Pax Judea : capital seeks growth venues ; Israel as hub for infrastructure / AI / surveillance in a destabilized Middle East (speaker) ; host raises overextension / domestic crisis counter.
