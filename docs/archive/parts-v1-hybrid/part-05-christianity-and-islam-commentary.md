## Law-discovery answer (draft)

**Question:** How does sacred legitimacy migrate across revelations and empires?

**Working answer:** Part V tracks **textual → missionary → imperial → institutional → revolutionary** transformations of sacred authority. Jesus (`civ-24`) splits into **historical**, **biblical**, and **Gnostic** source layers; martyrdom and original-sin theology clash with the Yahwist grammar from Part IV. **Paul** (`civ-25`) — not Jesus in the lecture's frame — transforms the movement into **Christianity**: Gentile opening, faith-not-works, Roman protection, and a high-risk **spy** reading. **Constantine/Nicaea** (`civ-26`) weld doctrine to empire; the **godhead** becomes monotheism as **closed reality-order**, with lecture extensions toward money, science, and nation-state. **Augustine** (`civ-27`) lifts the Church **out of history** after Rome's sack — City of God, obedience doctrine, eastward dissent. **Muhammad/Islam** (`civ-28`) answers with Abrahamic revolutionary universalism, land/debt/jihad framing, and imperial **whitewashing** after victory. Meta-pattern: legitimacy migrates by re-authoring who speaks for God and which institution survives regime collapse.

### civ-24

**Source-layer opener** after [Part IV `civ-23`](./part-04-ancient-foundations-commentary.md#civ-23). **Historical Jesus:** Galilee (~4 BCE), John the Baptist, healing ministry, Roman crucifixion (~30–33 CE). **Biblical Jesus:** Son of God, virgin birth, twelve apostles, Kingdom of Heaven, forgiveness, torn curtain, atonement. Lecture treats these as **different objects** — reconstruction vs theological narration.

**Yahwist clash:** original-sin theology vs [Part IV `civ-22`](./part-04-ancient-foundations-commentary.md#civ-22) Adam/Eve (God fallible, domestic comedy, faith-as-argument). **Responsibility:** "Jews killed Jesus" complicated — Sadducees/Pharisees/Essenes distinctions; **Pilate** as violent Roman authority. **Gnostic layer:** secret/inner/public teachings; Gospel of Thomas; Monad/Sophia; monstrous Yahweh; **divine spark** trapped in matter. **Martyrdom:** Roman execution → hero transformation. Closing claim: **Paul**, not Jesus, founds Christianity. **Downstream:** Paul cluster (`civ-25`).

### civ-25

**Jesus–Christianity paradox:** kingdom **within us** (lecture's Jesus) vs Son of God, atonement, second coming (Christianity). **Paul** as hinge: Roman **citizen**, Pharisee, Damascus conversion, missionary networks, **faith not works**, circumcision **optional** for Gentiles, **churches** as portable cells. **Rival lines:** James the Just (Jesus's brother); **Ebionites** (lecture forward-link toward Islam). **Acts** ending in Rome — preaching without hindrance under **Roman protection**; politically load-bearing.

**Paul-as-spy thesis** (highest Part V risk): lecture argues Paul neutralizes Jewish **fanaticism** by (1) relaxing purity/circumcision, (2) invoking Roman citizenship against persecution, (3) declaring Messiah already came. **Pauline revolutions:** faith-as-belief, miracles, **tradition over Bible**, later Catholic Bible restriction. **Tolstoy Lens** (chapter adjunct): bounds spy thesis — Paul matters, but Roman roads/law/cities enable portability. **Downstream:** Constantine cluster (`civ-26`).

Lecture's **modernity extension** (guardrailed): capitalism, science, nation-state as successors where **symbols become reality** and expansion is necessary to make the system true. **Downstream:** Augustine sack-crisis (`civ-27`).
