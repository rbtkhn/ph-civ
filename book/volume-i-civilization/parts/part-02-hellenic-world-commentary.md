## Law-discovery answer (draft)

**Question:** How does epic → institution → theater → philosophy → conquest-carrier build a civilizational grammar?

**Working answer:** Greek civilization in this arc is not a continuous Bronze Age inheritance but a **post-collapse recombination**. Homer supplies the imaginative grammar (empathy, psychology, metaphor, poet as founder). The polis and `eudaimonia` turn that ethic into competitive institutions (`civ-08`). Theater makes moral education public (`civ-09`). Philosophy internalizes and challenges democracy on the page (`civ-10`). Macedon converts the grammar into **conquest-carrier** infrastructure—Philip's pan-Hellenic project, Alexander's overreach, then Hellenistic standardization (`civ-11`–`civ-13`). Poet-legislator doctrine (`gb-03`) names the mechanism: language and shared imagination precede empire.

## Hellenic grammar (mechanism spine)

1. **Epic** — Homer reshapes imagination; Iliad as empathy/psychology school (`civ-07`; deepened `gb-03`).
2. **Institution** — Polis, alphabet, oral→literary shift; eudaimonia and competitive democracy (`civ-07`, `civ-08`).
3. **Theater** — Playwrights as democratic teachers; hubris/catharsis (`civ-09`; Shelley frame in `gb-03`).
4. **Philosophy** — Socrates/Plato challenge democracy; dialogue on the page (`civ-10`).
5. **Conquest-carrier** — Philip II pan-Hellenic project; Alexander's expansion; Hellenistic sync and Alexandria standardization (`civ-11`–`civ-13`).
