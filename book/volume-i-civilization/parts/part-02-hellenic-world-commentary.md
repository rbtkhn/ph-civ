## Law-discovery answer (draft)

**Question:** How does epic ‚Üí institution ‚Üí theater ‚Üí philosophy ‚Üí conquest-carrier build a civilizational grammar?

**Working answer:** Greek civilization in this arc is not a continuous Bronze Age inheritance but a **post-collapse recombination**. Homer supplies the imaginative grammar (empathy, psychology, metaphor, poet as founder). The polis and `eudaimonia` turn that ethic into competitive institutions (`civ-08`). Theater makes moral education public (`civ-09`). Philosophy internalizes and challenges democracy on the page (`civ-10`). Macedon converts the grammar into **conquest-carrier** infrastructure‚ÄîPhilip's pan-Hellenic project, Alexander's overreach, then Hellenistic standardization (`civ-11`‚Äì`civ-13`). Poet-legislator doctrine (`gb-03`) names the mechanism: language and shared imagination precede empire.
