## Law-discovery answer (draft)

**Question:** What parallel grammars sit beside Greece/Rome on the ancient worlds floor?

**Working answer:** After Greece/Rome in the interwoven spine, the course lays a **comparative ancient floor** — not prelude but parallel grammars answering mortality, order, and legitimacy differently. Egypt (`civ-18`) builds **monument-eternity** and state-scale awe; Mesopotamia (`civ-19`) answers through **myth and Gilgamesh** against pyramid permanence; Indus (`civ-20`) offers **urban order without readable scripture**; the Levant block (`civ-21`–`23`) turns writing into **apology, cosmology, and imperial-religious merger** — Bible as political real estate, Yahwist literary genius, Cyrus/Ezra/Zoroastrian transformation toward Christianity. The Part's meta-pattern: ancient civilization as comparative field (monument, myth, city, text) — do not collapse into one cause.
