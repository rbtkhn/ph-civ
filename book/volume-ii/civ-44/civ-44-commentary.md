## Layer 1 - Neutral Summary

The lecture opens the Age of Exploration by placing Spanish conquest inside longer global interconnection. Jiang links Islamic and Mongol trade networks, paper, gunpowder, maritime exchange, and earlier Viking contact to the eventual collision of Old and New World societies.

The middle of the chapter turns to the Spanish, Aztecs, Mayans, and Incas. Conquistadors are presented as marginal and violent adventurers, while Indigenous societies are treated as complex imperial orders with their own hierarchy, religious worlds, military forms, and vulnerabilities.

The ending stresses worldview collapse. Jiang argues that conquest worked not only through weapons or numbers, but through disease, hierarchy, taboo-breaking, killing or capturing sacred figures, and the psychological destruction of a religious operating system.

- **New World rupture:** the collision of Old World and American societies after older patterns of interconnection.
- **Conquistador logic:** conquest by marginal armed outsiders seeking opportunity and status.
