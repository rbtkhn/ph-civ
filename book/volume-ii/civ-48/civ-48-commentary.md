## Layer 1 - Neutral Summary

The lecture completes the French Revolution trilogy by arguing that Napoleon would not have been possible without Robespierre. Jiang contrasts the rare individual genius of a battlefield commander with the rarer political capacity to promote talent and build a system.

Napoleon is treated as both a military leader and a myth-maker. The lecture connects revolutionary disruption, meritocratic opening, institutional promotion, and charismatic empire, showing how the Revolution creates conditions Napoleon can exploit and embody.

The ending moves into modern analogy and national myth. Those analogies are useful for tracking the lecture's model of political imagination, but they require extra caution because they are date-sensitive and rhetorically charged.
