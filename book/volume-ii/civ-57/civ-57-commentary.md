## Layer 1 - Neutral Summary
The lecture places Freud inside a long religious and intellectual sequence, moving from animism, mother goddess religion, polytheism, monotheism, Catholic mediation, Protestant crisis of faith, Kant, Hegel, and Marx into psychoanalysis.

Freud is presented as a radical turn inward. Jiang describes the id, ego, superego, Oedipus and Electra complexes, Jung's collective unconscious, archetypes, persona, shadow, and self as attempts to explain individual psychology and modern identity.

The later argument treats Freud and modernism as bound to trauma, sexuality, secrecy, elite protection, city life, abstraction, alienation, disenchantment, and the rise of a culture organized around the self. The lecture's claims are ambitious and high-risk, so the commentary keeps psychoanalytic, sexual, religious, and modernism-causation claims visibly bounded.
