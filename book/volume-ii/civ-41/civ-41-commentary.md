## Layer 1 - Neutral Summary

The lecture asks how the Renaissance began and defines it as a re-imagining of classical Greece inside Christian Europe. Jiang places Italian city-state competition, contact with the Muslim world, Byzantine decline, artistic patronage, and publishing-like networks around a central claim: Dante is the decisive spark.

The middle of the chapter presents Dante as a creator of modern individuality and human self-expression. Renaissance figures such as Petrarch, Boccaccio, Leonardo, and Michelangelo are read as participants in a world that Dante made imaginatively possible.

The ending elevates love and imagination into the chapter's central civilizational forces. Jiang interprets Michelangelo's Creation of Adam through Dante, arguing that imagination gives life to the world and that Dante's poetic revolution could transform the Catholic Church from within.
